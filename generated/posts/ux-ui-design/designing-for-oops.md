# Designing for Oops

## AI Summary
The post explores how human error is inevitable and how systems often fail by assuming perfection. It differentiates between 'slips' (action errors) and 'mistakes' (mental model errors) and emphasizes proactive design principles like poka-yoke to mitigate them. Examples from aviation safety, manufacturing, and consumer products illustrate how error-proofing works, while critiquing healthcare's lack of adoption despite high stakes. The article advocates for cultural shifts in error reporting and design thinking to create safer, more forgiving systems.
