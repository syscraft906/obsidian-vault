# Flawed Products Harm –  A Framework to Respond

## AI Summary
This blog post highlights how flawed technology products can cause significant harm to underserved groups, including racial minorities, the disabled, and women. It defines flawed products as those developed without understanding users' diverse needs and provides concrete examples like biased healthcare algorithms and problematic Slack Connect features. The author introduces the '3Q-Do No Harm Framework'—Three Questions to proactively address harms through inclusive design practices, systemic risk analysis, and robust problem resolution strategies. The framework emphasizes team accountability and iterative improvements to mitigate technology's negative societal impacts.
