# When AI experiences fail, who is held accountable?

## AI Summary
This article explores the complex issue of accountability in AI systems when they cause harm to users. It highlights several real-world cases where AI systems failed, including incorrect advice from chatbots and discriminatory hiring algorithms. The piece emphasizes how diffusion of responsibility within organizations makes holding anyone accountable difficult, and discusses the role of designers in shaping AI interfaces, even though they often lack authority over deployment decisions. It calls for a clearer professional framework and accountability standards for designers working with AI to prevent harm.
