# Tipping: Two apps, two philosophies

## AI Summary
The blog post compares Uber Eats and Bolt Food's tipping systems, highlighting how their architectural choices create distinct trade-offs in courier earnings, customer behavior, and platform trust. Uber Eats allows post-delivery tip adjustments, maximizing tips but enabling abuse, while Bolt Food locks tips at checkout to prevent manipulation at the cost of lower average tipping. Data and experiments reveal a tension between flexibility (higher tips but more fraud) and rigidity (fewer issues but suppressed generosity). Regional behavioral differences, payment infrastructure constraints, and the emotional impact of 'tip baiting' underscore how design decisions shape economic outcomes. The analysis emphasizes that tipping systems are not just UX choices but foundational product philosophy with cascading consequences.
