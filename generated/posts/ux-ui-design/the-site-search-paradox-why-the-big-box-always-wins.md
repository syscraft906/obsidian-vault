# The Site-Search Paradox: Why The Big Box Always Wins

## AI Summary
This article explores the 'Site-Search Paradox,' where users often prefer Google over internal site search due to poor implementation of information architecture (IA) and user-centric design. It highlights how traditional site search relies on exact string matches rather than semantic understanding, leading to high exit rates and lost conversions. The author provides a four-phase audit framework and practical UX strategies to improve search by addressing synonym gaps, fuzzy matching, and intent-based filtering. Case studies demonstrate the impact of aligning metadata with user language, emphasizing that better IA—not just technical fixes—is key to solving search issues. The piece concludes with a checklist for creating a search experience that guides users toward their goals without penalizing natural language use or typos.
