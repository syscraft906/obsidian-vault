# 100 More Things #191: AS PEOPLE AGE, THEY BECOME LESS CONFIDENT ABOUT THEIR OWN MEMORIES

## AI Summary
This blog post challenges the conventional belief that aging directly causes memory decline, citing research by Dayna Touron (2015) showing older adults retain memory capabilities but lack confidence in their cognitive abilities. Studies reveal that older adults may rely more on external tools (e.g., GPS) or repetitive checking (e.g., math calculations) due to low confidence in their memory accuracy and efficiency. The author emphasizes that this self-doubt, rather than diminished memory, often explains slower task completion in older populations, with takeaways including the need to encourage trust in memory use.
