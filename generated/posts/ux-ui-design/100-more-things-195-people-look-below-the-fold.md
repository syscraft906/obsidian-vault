# 100 More Things #195: PEOPLE LOOK BELOW “THE FOLD”

## AI Summary
The blog post explores the usability debate around scrolling and pagination in web design, referencing data from Chartbeat and ClickTale to show that 66-76% of users scroll beyond 'the fold' on websites. It clarifies the origin of 'the fold' from newspaper design and provides actionable takeaways, emphasizing that vertical scrolling is accepted by users but horizontal scrolling should be avoided. The key advice includes placing critical content above the fold while ensuring engaging, continuous content to drive further scrolling and avoiding 'dead zones' in layouts.
