# Anime vs. Marvel/DC: Designing Digital Products With Emotion In Flow

## AI Summary
This blog post explores how emotional flow in digital product design can be inspired by anime's seamless transitions between tones, contrasting with Marvel/DC films' tonal missteps. It defines 'emotion in flow' (coherent, telegraphed emotional shifts) and 'emotion in conflict' (jarring, disruptive tone clashes), applying these concepts to UX through Don Norman's emotional design layers and microinteractions. Practical examples and strategies—like using beat sheets, aligning tone with task risk, and testing emotional continuity—are provided to help designers create immersive, stress-free user experiences.
