# Who can actually afford AI tools now?

## AI Summary
The blog post examines the growing trend of credit-based pricing models in AI-driven SaaS tools, highlighting how companies like Figma and Cursor introduced free features, established user dependency, and later monetized them through opaque credit systems. It critiques the disproportionate impact on freelancers and small teams, who face restricted access due to unpredictable costs and limited budgets. The post also explores industry-wide adoption of such models, ethical concerns around pricing transparency, and the psychological effects of disrupted workflows. It concludes that while AI costs are real, current pricing structures often prioritize profit over user equity.
