# 100 More Things #198: PEOPLE PROCESS SENSORY DATA UNCONSCIOUSLY

## AI Summary
The blog post explores sensory substitution and addition, where the brain processes environmental data through alternative senses like touch, often unconsciously. It details experiments using vests to translate audio or stock market data into tactile patterns, allowing users to interpret words or make data-driven decisions without conscious awareness. The post also introduces the eXperience Induction Machine (XIM), a sensory-rich room for immersive data visualization, and emphasizes practical applications in accessibility and big data analysis.
