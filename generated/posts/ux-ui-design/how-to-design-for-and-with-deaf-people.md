# How To Design For (And With) Deaf People

## AI Summary
This article challenges stereotypes about deaf individuals and emphasizes inclusive design practices. It explains the spectrum of hearing loss, highlights the importance of respecting self-identified terminology (e.g., Deaf vs. deaf vs. hard of hearing), and provides practical UX guidelines for accessible communication. Key recommendations include using text alternatives, haptic feedback, circular seating layouts, and involving the deaf community in product testing. The post underscores that accessibility benefits all users and requires deliberate efforts to avoid exclusion.
